<p class="about-lede">I’m Andreas Kennedy Rønning. I’ve spent more than twenty years building, modernizing, and occasionally coercing complex systems into doing useful things.</p>

I started as a graphics designer and animator, became a Flash developer almost by accident, and kept following interesting problems through games, interactive exhibitions, visual programming, native and cross-platform tooling, enterprise integrations, and secure public-sector software.

The common thread is not a particular platform or industry. It is a fondness for the places where disciplines meet: design and engineering, tools and their users, visual expression and formal systems, ambitious ideas and the inconvenient facts of production.

I love language, expression, and collaboration, and I’m proud to call myself a forever amateur. The work of self-improvement is never complete. This site is where I collect musings, strong opinions, video games, music, enterprise software, and anything else that refuses to fit neatly on a résumé.

## A few things I’ve done

- Led development of an interactive, real-time view into secure healthcare planning data for the Norwegian public sector.
- Built safety and location-tracking companion applications for security personnel working in the field.
- Created developer tooling that lets web developers contribute TypeScript and React frontends to ServiceNow projects without first becoming platform specialists.
- Helped build Fuse’s cross-platform UX toolchain and worked across C#, C++, Objective-C, Java, JavaScript, TypeScript, Uno, and .UX.
- Designed visual programming and particle-system tools that enabled non-technical authors to create interactive content.
- Integrated sensor networks, computer vision, and high-performance graphics into installations for museums and cultural institutions.
- Built a networked, 20-plus-player exhibit teaching diplomacy and collaborative problem-solving through a shared simulated crisis.

## Speaking, tools, and open source

I created [**DConsole**](https://github.com/furusystems/DConsole), an extensible developer console that brought real-time introspection and debugging to Flash and AIR projects. I’ve contributed to the Haxe ecosystem, presented three sessions at **Flash on the Beach** and **Reasons to be Creative** in Brighton—including a surviving [three-minute elevator pitch](https://vimeo.com/15740065)—and delivered international product demonstrations and workshops for Fuse.

Much of the older work survives on [GitHub](https://github.com/sunjammer): Haxe bindings, graphics experiments, game fragments, odd utilities, and repositories whose names seemed perfectly reasonable at the time. Newer professional writing also appears on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/andreas-kennedy-r%C3%B8nning-ab823867/). Music lives on [Bandcamp](https://sunjammer.bandcamp.com/) and [SoundCloud](https://soundcloud.com/doomsday).
